Quackers was no ordinary duck. Each dawn he greeted the lake with philosophical questions—why do ripples travel, why do clouds wander? One misty morning he discovered that the loudest "quack" isn’t always the one that’s heard; kindness echoed further across the water than any sound. By sunset, every creature—frog, heron, even the bashful carp—had joined his gentle chorus. And so the smallest duck taught the largest lake that wisdom often comes wrapped in feathers.
